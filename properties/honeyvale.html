In the coffee

A planter's bungalow with a long memory.

Honeyvale is a restored estate bungalow — red oxide floors, gabled tin-and-tile roof, monkey-tops over the windows and a deep verandah looking down the lawn to the hills. It has been lived in and loved for the better part of a century.

Six acres of coffee and shade-trees surround it. Mornings smell of woodsmoke and blossom; evenings are for the fireplace and a long table.

This is the real Malnad — birdsong, mist, and the unhurried rhythm of a working estate.
